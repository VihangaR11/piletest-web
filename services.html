High Strain Dynamic Testing · ASTM D4945

Dynamic Pile Testing (PDA)

High Strain Dynamic Load Testing is a fast, reliable and cost-effective method of determining carrying capacity of piles as an alternative to static load test. Performed using the Pile Driving Analyzer (PDA-8G), Smart Strain gauges, Smart Accelerometers and CAPWAP® software.

  • Structural Integrity assessment
  • Skin Friction and End Bearing distribution
  • Driving Stress and hammer performance
  • Load settlement curve generation
  • CAPWAP® post-processing for each pile
  • Load range: 250 kN to 75,000 kN
  • Own hammers up to 80 Ton with guide frames

We use the Pile Driving Analyzer (PDA®) system developed by Pile Dynamics Inc., USA — the most widely employed system for Dynamic Load Testing and Pile Driving Monitoring in the world.

Low Strain Integrity Testing · ASTM D5882

Pile Integrity Testing (PIT)

PIT performs Low Strain integrity testing using the Pile Integrity Tester Model PIT-QV. A small impact is administered to the pile top and the acoustic wave propagates down through the pile. Reflected waves are recorded and analyzed using PIT-W® software.

  • Bored and cast in-situ concrete piles
  • Auger cast piles and concrete filled pipe piles
  • Detects cracks, voids, and diameter reductions
  • Quick and economical — verify all piles on site
  • Time domain and frequency domain analysis
  • Transient Response (TR) method available
  • Mobility spectrum and impedance profiles

The Pulse Echo Integrity Testing is a non-destructive pile testing method that verifies the integrity and provides graphical representation using PIT-W® software.

Ultrasonic Testing · ASTM D6760

Cross-Hole Sonic Logging (CSL)

CSL assures the absence of cracks and voids and verifies concrete quality on shafts built with inspection tubes. The test equipment consists of ultrasonic probes in the 40–60 kHz range. One probe emits a pulse while the other records sound waves — measuring First Arrival Time (FAT).

  • Drilled shafts, slurry walls, bored piles
  • Minimum 3 tubes (2-inch ID) per pile
  • Tubes filled with water as coupling medium
  • Waterfall diagram from FAT measurements
  • Complemented by Tomography analysis
  • Standardized in USA (ASTM), France (AFNOR)

Maintained Load Test · BS 8004

Static Load Testing (SLT / MLT)

The pile is tested by applying a direct load through a hydraulic jack against a Kentledge while monitoring settlement at pile top. The Kentledge is prepared using steel girders. The maximum load depends on the working load applied on the pile in axial compression or tension.

  • Load range: 100 kN to 40,000 kN
  • Kentledge using steel girders
  • 1000 MT hydraulic jacks
  • Settlement monitoring at pile top
  • The gold standard of pile performance
  • Compression and tension testing

Environmental Monitoring · DIN 4150

Vibration Monitoring (VM)

Continuous vibration and noise monitoring during pile driving and related construction activities. During each time interval, the monitoring system measures both the maximum vibration levels and frequencies in x-, y- and z-directions.

  • X, Y, Z directional measurement
  • Transverse, Vertical, Longitudinal components
  • Frequency (Hz) and Peak (mm/s) recording
  • Hourly full signal recording of highest peak
  • Digital signal processing for high accuracy
  • Real-time community impact assessment

Instrumented Testing

Instrument Maintained Load Test (IMLT)

Instrumented static pile load tests to determine soil parameters, verify and optimize foundation design. Embedded strain gauge instrumentation is monitored for each loading cycle to calculate axial loads along the pile shaft.

  • Minimize risk from ground condition uncertainties
  • Optimize pile design (size, length, factor of safety)
  • Confirm pile installation criteria
  • Tell-tale extension meters for deflection measurement
  • Strain gauges for axial load distribution
  • Load range: 100 kN to 40,000 kN
