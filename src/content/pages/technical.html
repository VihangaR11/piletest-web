Technology reference

From Measured Signals to Engineering Insight

Pile Dynamics systems support several stages of deep-foundation quality assurance. The equipment records the response; trained engineers interpret the measurements in the context of the pile, soil and test conditions.

01High strain

PDA® field measurements

Accelerometers and strain transducers record the pile response to impact. The system supports assessment of capacity, structural response, driving stresses and hammer performance.

Impact→Force & velocity→Field results
Official PDA reference ↗
02Signal matching

CAPWAP® post-processing

Force and velocity records from dynamic testing are signal-matched to estimate total resistance, its distribution along the shaft and toe, and the predicted load-displacement response.

PDA record→Signal match→Resistance model
Official CAPWAP reference ↗
03Low strain

PIT pulse-echo screening

A light impact generates a stress wave through the foundation. Reflections can indicate possible changes such as major cracks, necking, inclusions or voids and may assist with pile-length assessment.

Light tap→Wave reflection→Integrity review
Official PIT reference ↗
04Ultrasonic

CHAMP cross-hole logging

Ultrasonic pulses travel between water-filled access tubes in concrete foundations. Arrival time and signal energy help engineers assess concrete uniformity between each tested tube pair.

Tube pair→Sonic pulse→Concrete profile
