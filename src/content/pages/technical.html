Testing systems

Primary Equipment & Instrumentation

Field systems used to measure foundation response, integrity, construction vibration and ground movement.

PDA®-8G
Pile Driving Analyzer 8G
A specialist system for dynamic load testing and pile-driving monitoring, developed by Pile Dynamics Inc., USA. It uses strain and acceleration measurements to support testing in accordance with ASTM D4945. Listed load range: 250 kN to 75,000 kN, with hammers up to 80 tonnes.
CAPABILITIES
Bearing Capacity Shaft Integrity Driving Stress CAPWAP® Analysis
PIT-QV
Pile Integrity Tester
Low-strain sonic echo/pulse-echo testing for concrete and timber foundations. Measured responses support screening for major cracks, voids and diameter changes in accordance with ASTM D5882. Includes PIT-W® analysis software.
CHAMP
Cross-Hole Analyzer
Ultrasonic probes 40–60 kHz range. Cross-hole and single-hole sonic logging. Digital encoder for depth recording. Water-coupled acoustic measurements. ASTM D6760.
IMLT
Instrumented Load Test System
Displacement transducers, strain gauges, mechanical tell-tale extensometers, load cells and data loggers. 100 kN to 40,000 kN range.
VMS
Vibration Monitoring System
Continuous X/Y/Z vibration monitoring with frequency (Hz) and peak particle velocity (mm/s) recording. Measurements can be assessed against project criteria and references such as DIN 4150.
INC
Inclinometers & Piezometers
Lateral movement detection for retaining structures. Borehole installation for permanent monitoring. Ground water pressure measurement.
Analysis software

Measurement & Interpretation Tools

Specialist applications support field acquisition, signal processing and engineering interpretation.

CAPWAP®
Case Pile Wave Analysis Program. Estimates total bearing capacity, resistance distribution along shaft and at toe. Simulates static load test.
GRLWEAP
Wave equation analysis program for pile driving prediction. Optimizes hammer selection, driving criteria and pile installation.
PIT-W®
Pile Integrity Test analysis software. Graphical representation of velocity signals. Time domain and frequency domain analysis.
iCAP
Real-time data collection and analysis for PDA testing. Integrated capacity assessment during pile driving operations.

Technology reference

From Measured Signals to Engineering Insight

Pile Dynamics systems support several stages of deep-foundation quality assurance. The equipment records the response; trained engineers interpret the measurements in the context of the pile, soil and test conditions.

01High strain

PDA® field measurements

Accelerometers and strain transducers record the pile response to impact. The system supports assessment of capacity, structural response, driving stresses and hammer performance.

Impact→Force & velocity→Field results
Official PDA reference ↗
02Signal matching

CAPWAP® post-processing

Force and velocity records from dynamic testing are signal-matched to estimate total resistance, its distribution along the shaft and toe, and the predicted load-displacement response.

PDA record→Signal match→Resistance model
Official CAPWAP reference ↗
03Low strain

PIT pulse-echo screening

A light impact generates a stress wave through the foundation. Reflections can indicate possible changes such as major cracks, necking, inclusions or voids and may assist with pile-length assessment.

Light tap→Wave reflection→Integrity review
Official PIT reference ↗
04Ultrasonic

CHAMP cross-hole logging

Ultrasonic pulses travel between water-filled access tubes in concrete foundations. Arrival time and signal energy help engineers assess concrete uniformity between each tested tube pair.

Tube pair→Sonic pulse→Concrete profile
