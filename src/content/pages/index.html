How Foundation Testing Works

A clear, independent process that helps project teams understand how a deep foundation is performing.

  1. 01🏗️

    Foundation Prepared

    The pile and work area are prepared for the selected testing method.

  2. 02📡

    Equipment Installed

    Calibrated sensors and instruments are positioned by the field team.

  3. 03〽️

    Measurements Collected

    Response, movement, sound or load data are recorded during the test.

  4. 04🔎

    Engineer Review

    Experienced engineers examine the measurements and relevant project details.

  5. 05📋

    Report Issued

    The findings are organized into a clear technical report for the project team.

Common questions

Pile Testing, Explained Simply

Short answers for clients, neighbours, students and anyone curious about foundation quality assurance.

Ask the Team →
What is a pile foundation?

A pile is a long foundation element that transfers structural loads to stronger soil or rock deeper below the ground.

Why are piles tested?

Testing provides measured information about integrity, capacity, movement or concrete quality so engineers can assess foundation performance.

Does pile testing damage the pile?

Many methods, including PIT and CSL, are non-destructive. Load-testing arrangements vary, so the appropriate method is selected for the project.

How long does testing take?

Duration depends on the method, pile quantity, access and site readiness. The team confirms requirements when reviewing a project enquiry.

What is needed to request a quotation?

Useful information includes the site location, pile type, diameter, length, quantity, expected load, drawings, specification and preferred programme.

Where does PileTeST work?

The company serves construction projects across Sri Lanka from its offices in the Colombo area.

Useful terms

Foundation Testing Glossary

Select a term for a plain-language explanation.

Pile

A deep foundation element that carries structural load into the ground.

Bearing capacity

The load a foundation and surrounding ground can support under defined conditions.

Integrity

The continuity and general condition of the pile shaft.

Settlement

Downward movement of a foundation or structure.

Toe reflection

A signal response associated with the lower end of a pile during low-strain testing.

Geotechnical investigation

Field and laboratory work used to understand soil, rock and groundwater conditions.
