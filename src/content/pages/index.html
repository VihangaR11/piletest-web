Common questions

Pile Testing, Explained Simply

Short answers for clients, consultants, contractors and anyone learning about foundation quality assurance.

Ask the Team →
What is a pile foundation?

A pile is a long foundation element that transfers structural loads to stronger soil or rock deeper below the ground.

Why are piles tested?

Testing provides measured information about integrity, capacity, movement or concrete quality so engineers can assess foundation performance.

Does pile testing damage the pile?

Many methods, including PIT and CSL, are non-destructive. Load-testing arrangements vary, so the appropriate method is selected for the project.

How long does testing take?

Duration depends on the method, pile quantity, access and site readiness. The team confirms requirements when reviewing a project enquiry.

What is needed to request a quotation?

Useful information includes the site location, pile type, diameter, length, quantity, expected load, drawings, specification and preferred programme.

Where does PileTeST work?

The company serves construction projects across Sri Lanka from its offices in the Colombo area.
