High Strain Dynamic Testing · ASTM D4945

Dynamic Pile Testing (PDA)

High-strain dynamic load testing provides a rapid assessment of pile capacity, driving stresses and hammer performance. Testing is carried out using a Pile Driving Analyzer (PDA-8G), smart strain transducers, accelerometers and CAPWAP® analysis software.

  • Structural Integrity assessment
  • Skin Friction and End Bearing distribution
  • Driving Stress and hammer performance
  • Load settlement curve generation
  • CAPWAP® post-processing for each pile
  • Load range: 250 kN to 75,000 kN
  • Impact hammers up to 80 tonnes with guide frames

We use the Pile Driving Analyzer (PDA®) system developed by Pile Dynamics Inc., USA — the most widely employed system for Dynamic Load Testing and Pile Driving Monitoring in the world.

Low Strain Integrity Testing · ASTM D5882

Pile Integrity Testing (PIT)

Low-strain integrity testing uses the PIT-QV Pile Integrity Tester. A small impact at the pile head generates a wave that travels along the pile; reflected signals are recorded and interpreted using PIT-W® software.

  • Bored and cast in-situ concrete piles
  • Auger cast piles and concrete filled pipe piles
  • Detects cracks, voids, and diameter reductions
  • Quick and economical — verify all piles on site
  • Time domain and frequency domain analysis
  • Transient Response (TR) method available
  • Mobility spectrum and impedance profiles

The pulse-echo method is a non-destructive screening technique used to identify possible changes in pile section or material consistency and present the recorded response graphically.

Ultrasonic Testing · ASTM D6760

Cross-Hole Sonic Logging (CSL)

CSL evaluates concrete consistency and helps identify possible anomalies in shafts constructed with access tubes. Ultrasonic probes operate in transmitter-receiver pairs, recording signal arrival time and energy as they move through the tubes.

  • Drilled shafts, slurry walls, bored piles
  • Minimum 3 tubes (2-inch ID) per pile
  • Tubes filled with water as coupling medium
  • Waterfall diagram from FAT measurements
  • Complemented by Tomography analysis
  • Standardized in USA (ASTM), France (AFNOR)

Maintained Load Test · BS 8004

Static Load Testing (SLT / MLT)

A controlled axial load is applied through hydraulic jacks against a designed reaction system while pile-head movement is measured. The loading schedule and maximum test load are selected for the pile design and the applicable project specification.

  • Load range: 100 kN to 40,000 kN
  • Kentledge using steel girders
  • 1000 MT hydraulic jacks
  • Settlement monitoring at pile top
  • The gold standard of pile performance
  • Compression and tension testing

Environmental Monitoring · DIN 4150

Vibration Monitoring (VM)

Continuous vibration and noise monitoring during pile driving and related construction activities. During each time interval, the monitoring system measures both the maximum vibration levels and frequencies in x-, y- and z-directions.

  • X, Y, Z directional measurement
  • Transverse, Vertical, Longitudinal components
  • Frequency (Hz) and Peak (mm/s) recording
  • Hourly full signal recording of highest peak
  • Digital signal processing for high accuracy
  • Real-time community impact assessment

Instrumented Testing

Instrumented Maintained Load Test (IMLT)

Instrumented static pile load tests to determine soil parameters, verify and optimize foundation design. Embedded strain gauge instrumentation is monitored for each loading cycle to calculate axial loads along the pile shaft.

  • Minimize risk from ground condition uncertainties
  • Optimize pile design (size, length, factor of safety)
  • Confirm pile installation criteria
  • Tell-tale extension meters for deflection measurement
  • Strain gauges for axial load distribution
  • Load range: 100 kN to 40,000 kN

Supporting capability

Additional Engineering Services

Complementary analysis, field testing and monitoring can be selected to suit the foundation system, site conditions and project requirements.

CAPWAP Analysis

Signal-matching analysis of dynamic pile-test data to estimate soil resistance distribution and pile response.

WEAP Analysis

Wave-equation assessment used to support pile-driving system selection and drivability evaluation.

TOMOSONIC Analysis

Tomographic processing of cross-hole sonic logging data to investigate possible concrete anomalies.

Thermal Integrity Profiling

Thermal measurements used to assess concrete continuity and identify potential shaft irregularities.

Slope Stability Analysis

Engineering assessment of slope conditions and potential failure mechanisms for design decisions.

Foundation Design

Geotechnical input and foundation recommendations informed by ground conditions and structural demands.

Soil Resistivity Survey

Field measurement of soil electrical resistivity for earthing and infrastructure planning.

Plate Load Testing

In-situ measurement of near-surface bearing response and deformation under an applied plate load.

Lateral Load Testing

Controlled lateral loading to assess pile-head movement and lateral foundation performance.

Pullout Testing

Tension testing used to evaluate anchorage or foundation resistance under an applied pullout load.

Rebound Hammer Testing

Non-destructive surface-hardness screening to support comparative assessment of concrete.

Noise Level Monitoring

Construction-noise measurement and reporting for site control and environmental management.
