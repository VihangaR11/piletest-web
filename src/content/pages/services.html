01
Foundation verification

Pile Testing Services

Measure pile capacity, integrity, concrete consistency and movement under controlled loading.

ASTM D4945PDA

High Strain Dynamic Load Testing

Rapid assessment of pile capacity, driving stresses and hammer performance using the PDA-8G system and CAPWAP analysis.

  • 250-75,000 kN listed system range
  • Skin friction and end-bearing distribution
  • Driving stress and load-settlement review
View PDA equipment ->
BS 8004SLT

Static Load Testing

Controlled axial loading with hydraulic jacks and a designed reaction system to measure pile-head load and settlement response.

  • Compression and tension testing
  • 100-40,000 kN listed range
InstrumentedIMLT

Instrumented Static Load Testing

Embedded strain gauges and tell-tale instruments measure load transfer and displacement during each loading cycle.

Bi-directionalBDSLT

Bi-Directional Static Load Testing

Internal loading arrangements assess upward shaft response and downward toe response without a conventional surface reaction frame.

ASTM D5882PIT

Low Strain Pile Integrity Testing

Pulse-echo screening records wave reflections from a light impact to identify possible cracks, voids or changes in pile section.

  • PIT-QV equipment and PIT-W analysis
  • Time and frequency-domain review
  • 38,002 records through 2024
ASTM D6760CSL

Cross-Hole Sonic Logging

Ultrasonic measurements between access tubes assess concrete uniformity in bored piles, drilled shafts and diaphragm walls.

  • Arrival-time and signal-energy review
  • Tomographic analysis available
  • 8,057 records through 2024
Horizontal responseLLT

Lateral Load Testing

Controlled horizontal loading measures pile-head movement and lateral foundation response.

Tension responseULT

Uplift / Pullout Load Testing

Controlled tensile loading assesses pile or anchor resistance and movement under uplift action.

Physical verificationNX

NX Coring of Piles

Core recovery provides direct physical evidence of pile material and internal concrete condition where specified.

03
Structural assessment

Non-Destructive Testing

Screen concrete, reinforcement and welded components while minimizing damage to the tested element.

Concrete screeningRHT

Rebound Hammer Test

Surface-hardness measurements support comparative assessment of concrete uniformity.

Concrete transmissionUPV

Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity Test

Pulse travel time through concrete supports evaluation of material consistency and possible internal discontinuities.

Weld inspectionUT

Ultrasonic Testing of Welds

High-frequency sound is used to inspect weld volume for internal reflectors and discontinuities.

Surface inspectionMPI

Magnetic Particle Inspection

Magnetic fields and inspection particles reveal surface and near-surface discontinuities in ferromagnetic materials.

Surface inspectionLPI

Liquid Penetrant Inspection

Penetrant methods identify surface-breaking discontinuities in suitable non-porous materials.

Reinforcement surveyRCS

Rebar Cover Survey

Electronic scanning locates reinforcement and estimates concrete cover for structural assessment and planning.
