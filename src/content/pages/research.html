What is PIT-AI?

PIT-AI is a machine learning classifier developed as a Final Year Project in collaboration with PileTeST Consultants (PVT) LTD. It automatically analyses PIT (Pile Integrity Test) velocity graph images and classifies them into standard pile integrity categories.

The model uses physics-informed feature extraction — 43 signal processing features derived from velocity waveforms — trained on real field data from Sri Lanka's construction industry.

How It Works

📸
1. Upload Graph
Upload average velocity graph image from PIT-W® software output.
⚡
2. Extract Features
43 physics-informed features extracted: toe reflection, spectral entropy, energy distribution, peaks.
🤖
3. ML Classification
Random Forest Ensemble predicts class with probability score for each category.
✅
4. Safe Decision
High confidence → auto-classify. Low confidence → flag for engineer review.
Classification System

PIT Classification Reference

AA
Sound Shaft Integrity
Clear toe reflection at reported depth. Wave speed within acceptable range. Minor variations before toe — integrity confirmed.
AB
No Major Defect Indicated
No toe signal, no defect reflections. Acceptable integrity but depth uncertain. Does not confirm shaft is flawless over full length.
ABx
No Major Defect to Depth x
Method limitations prevent full length interpretation. Long piles or high soil resistance. No defect to depth x confirmed.
PFx
Probable Flaw at Depth x
Toe reflection visible PLUS reflection from unplanned reduction of size or material quality. Quantitative analysis recommended.
PDx
Probable Defect at Depth x
Strong reflection from major size/material reduction. No clear toe reflection — wave blocked by defect. Remediation may be required.
IR
Inconclusive Record
Poor pile top quality or low concrete strength. Tested too early. Retesting after waiting and/or pile top cleaning is advised.
⚠️ Important Disclaimer
PIT-AI is a research demonstration tool. All classifications must be verified by a certified geotechnical engineer before any construction or safety decisions. The model achieves 75.34% accuracy and is not approved for standalone use on life-critical structures.
